Choosing the right stream and subsequent career options after 12th Arts can be a difficult and often daunting task for students. The pressure to choose a career path that will lead to a successful and fulfilling life can be overwhelming. However, with a little bit of research and guidance, choosing the right career option after 12th Arts can be easy. Various career options are available to students looking for courses after 12th Arts. Some popular career options include teaching, social work, content writing, event management, and interior design. Each of these career options offers its own set of unique challenges and rewards.




How Do I Choose the Right Course After 12th Arts?

There are many factors to consider when choosing the best courses after 12th Arts. Some of the crucial factors include the following: -

  • Career Goals: If you want to choose the best course after 12th Arts, you must know what your long-term career goals are. What kind of job do you want to have after completing the course? Make sure the course you choose will help you achieve your goals.

  • Subjects Studied in 12th: What subjects did you study in 12th arts? If you have good marks in a particular subject, you may want to consider pursuing a course related to that subject.

  • Duration of the Course:How long is the course? Is it a full-time or part-time course? Make sure you can commit to the duration of the course.

  • Course Fee:Can you afford the course fees? Make sure you have considered all the costs associated with the course, such as textbooks, materials, and accommodation.

  • Location: Where is the course located? If you want to study in a particular city or country, make sure the course is offered in that location.

  • Reputation of the Institution:What is the reputation of the institution offering the course? Make sure to research the institution and read reviews from previous students.



Top Courses After 12th Arts

  2. Bachelor of Business Management [BMS]

    The Bachelor of Physical Education program is a four-year program that leads to a Bachelor of Science in Physical Education. The program is designed to prepare students for a career in fitness and wellness.

  4. Bachelor of Business Studies [BBS]

    Bachelor of Business Studies [BBS] is a 3-year undergraduate course offered by many universities in India. The course is designed to provide students with a sound understanding of business concepts and their applications in business.

Government Jobs After 12th Arts

  1. Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)

    After completing 12th Arts, students can start preparing for degree courses after 12th Arts like the UPSC exams. The UPSC conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE) every year to recruit candidates for the IAS, IPS, and other central government jobs. The CSE is a three-stage exam consisting of the Preliminary Examination (PE), Main Examination (ME), and Interview.

  2. National Defence Academy (NDA)

    Candidates who have completed the 12th Arts stream from a recognized board are eligible to apply for the NDA entrance examination. The examination is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). Candidates who clear the written examination are called for an interview and medical examination. The NDA course is among the professional courses after 12th humanities, which is a respectable career.

  3. SSC Combined Higher Secondary Level (CHSL)

    There are numerous career opportunities after 12th Arts. The SSC Combined Higher Secondary Level (CHSL) is a competitive examination conducted by the Staff Selection Commission to recruit various posts in the Government of India. The posts which are typically filled through the CHSL examination are those of Lower Divisional Clerk (LDC), Data Entry Operator (DEO), and Postal Assistant/Sorting Assistant (PA/SA).

  4. Indian Railway Recruitment Board (RRB)

    Government jobs after the 12th in Indian Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) are available in various positions like Junior Engineer, Assistant Loco Pilot, Group D, and others. The eligibility criteria for these jobs vary according to the position. RRB is among the most popular professional courses after 12th Arts.

  5. SSC Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)

    The SSC MTS or the Staff Selection Commission Multi-Tasking Staff is a government job that is available for 12th-pass students. The job is available in various government ministries and departments, and the selected candidate will be responsible for various tasks like office work, fieldwork, data entry, etc.

  6. SSC Stenographer (Grade C and Grade D)

    The SSC Stenographer (Grade C and Grade D) is a government job that requires a 12th SSC certificate. The job involves dictation from a senior official and transcribing it into a document. The job involves other secretarial duties, such as maintaining files and records and handling correspondence.

  7. Indian Coast Guard

    The Indian Coast Guard is a multi-mission organization, conducting round-the-year real-life operations at sea. Despite the odds, it has maintained high standards in its personnel and equipment quality. The Indian Coast Guard offers young men and women a challenging and inspiring career.

  8. Forest Guard

    The Forest Guard is responsible for maintaining and protecting forests and wildlife. They enforce laws and regulations related to using natural resources and work to prevent illegal activities such as hunting, logging, and pollution.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  1. What is the scope of Art?
    The job market for Arts degree holders is quite broad. Many find work in education, writing, publishing, the arts, marketing, and even non-profit organizations.

  2. Which job has the highest salary in Arts?
    The job with the highest salary after an arts degree is typically a CEO or other executive position.

  3. Can I become a software engineer with arts?
    You can become a software engineer with arts, but it will be difficult. You will need to take extra classes to make up for the classes you did not take in engineering.

  4. Can Arts students join an IT company?
    Yes, an arts student can join an IT company. However, they may not have the same skill set as someone who studied IT in school.
